Dear Editors of Physical Review A,

I am submitting the manuscript "Local number variance as a causal handle in the one-dimensional Bose–Hubbard chain" for consideration as a Regular Article.

The paper addresses whether a local structural observable — the site-resolved number variance F_i = <n_i^2> - <n_i>^2 — can serve as a targeted control handle in a driven-dissipative quantum lattice system. Concretely, does applying extra dephasing at the highest-F_i sites produce measurably more local occupation loss than applying the same dephasing budget at randomly chosen sites?

All evolution is exact Lindblad (no Trotter decomposition, no stochastic unravelling). The primary sweep over L ∈ {6, 7, 8}, J/U ∈ {0.12, 0.20, 0.30, 0.40}, and τ ∈ {1, 2, 3} reveals three regimes: F_i targeting is harmful at J/U = 0.12, transitional near J/U ≈ 0.20, and reliably beneficial at J/U ≥ 0.30. Best effects reach +0.047 to +0.072 (mean advantage) at J/U = 0.40, τ = 3.

Four disentanglement experiments rule out confounds that the primary sweep cannot separate — geometric centrality, shell position, mean occupation, disorder amplitude, and generator-action-based selection. In the clean, symmetric chain, F_i and geometric centrality are degenerate; symmetry breaking (disorder, deterministic tilt) separates them. Under strong disorder (μ_max = 2.0) F_i ranks first among nine tested selectors, and shell-matched permutation controls confirm the advantage is not reducible to shell membership. A gamma scan confirms the main choice γ_extra = 0.5 is near-optimal and not a tuning artefact.

This work fits Physical Review A as an exact many-body Lindblad study of a well-studied model (Bose–Hubbard) with a concrete, quantitative result on targeted dissipative control — a topic directly relevant to current experimental capabilities in cold-atom quantum simulation. The results are carefully scoped: the handle is regime-dependent, and no claims are made beyond the tested sizes (L ≤ 8) or selectors.

Code and raw data are publicly available at https://github.com/kunalb541/BH (MIT license).

I confirm that this manuscript has not been published elsewhere and is not under consideration at another journal.
