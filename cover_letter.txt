Dear Editors,

Please consider our manuscript, "Local number variance as a causal handle in the
one-dimensional Bose-Hubbard chain," for publication in Physical Review A.

The manuscript studies a targeted-intervention question in an open quantum lattice
system: under uniform Lindblad dephasing, can a local fluctuation observable identify
sites where additional dephasing produces a measurably different future response than
matched-budget random targeting? We address this in a one-dimensional Bose-Hubbard
chain using exact Lindblad evolution in fixed-particle-number sectors, with no Trotter
approximation or stochastic unraveling.

The main result is that local occupation variance,
F_i = <n_i^2> - <n_i>^2,
acts as a regime-dependent redistribution handle. In the tested finite chains
L in {6,7,8}, targeting the highest-F_i sites is harmful at J/U = 0.12, transitional
near J/U ~ 0.20, and robustly beneficial for J/U >= 0.30. The positive-pocket result
is stable across chain length and horizon.

The manuscript includes several controls designed to separate variance information from
simpler confounds. Disorder sweeps, shell-matched permutation controls, deterministic
tilt chains, and extra-dephasing-rate scans show that the effect is not reducible to
geometric centrality, shell position, mean occupation, disorder amplitude, or a tuned
intervention strength. Because the systems are small enough for complete enumeration,
we also rank the F_i-selected subset against all possible k-site intervention subsets;
in the positive-pocket regime it lies at the top of the subset-response distribution,
reaching global or near-global optimality across the tested conditions.

The mechanistic point of the paper is that F_i is not a local-depletion susceptibility.
Finite-difference response tests show instead that F_i tracks redistribution
susceptibility: high-F_i sites are those where a small extra dephasing dose most
strongly perturbs the global occupation pattern. This explains why the same local
observable can identify a strong response channel across regimes while the sign of the
local-loss outcome remains coupling-dependent.

The manuscript is intentionally scoped as an exact finite-size response study. It does
not claim thermodynamic-limit universality or optimal control. Rather, it establishes,
under controlled Lindblad dynamics, that local number variance can function as a
regime-dependent redistribution handle for targeted dephasing in finite Bose-Hubbard
chains.
